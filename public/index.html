Welcome to the Global Day of Coderetreat! December 14th, 2013

What is it?

Global Day of Coderetreat is a world-wide event celebrating passion and software craftsmanship. Last year, over 2000 passionate software developers in 150 cities around the world spent the day practicing the craft of software development using the coderetreat format. This year, our goal is to have 200 participating cities! With an average of 20-25 participants per city, that's over 3000 developers! You can find out more information about the day on the 2013 global day announcement.

Where is it?

Last year, we had 150 cities around the world (last year's map). This year, our goal is to have over 200 cities! We are currently in the process of registering cities. If you are interested in hosting an event in your city. please fill in the location registration form. As we register events, you can find out if there is one in your city on the official GDCR 2013 event list page. As official events are registered, we'll be adding to the map below.

We currently have more than 80 registered events!

Can I host a coderetreat?

Yes you can! We are glad you asked because we need your help to achieve our goal of 200 participating cities! To get started, fill in the location registration form and we will give you access to hosts-only mailing lists and other resources to help you plan your coderetreat.

Do you have questions about hosting a coderetreat, or are you unsure if you want to host? Feel free to check out our Hosting FAQ.

Who is organizing this?

Global Day of Coderetreat is an event organized by the Coderetreat Community Contribution Fund. Main organizers are Jim Hurne, Alissa Conaty, Adi Bolboaca, and Martin Klose.
